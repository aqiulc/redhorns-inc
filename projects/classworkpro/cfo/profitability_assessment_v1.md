### The Numbers Are Sensible (Despite Being Obscene)

**Why This Works Financially**:

1. **Zero Marginal Labor Cost**: Students work for "credit" not cash. This is the core economic engine that makes margins impossible for competitors to match.

2. **Institutional Distribution**: We don't acquire 100K students individually (CAC would be $500+ each). We acquire 250 schools at $10K each, who deliver 400 students per school. Amortized CAC: $25/student.

3. **Structural Retention**: Students can't drop out without failing course. Businesses become dependent on workflow. Schools are locked in by curriculum integration. Churn is structurally minimized.

4. **Network Effects**: More students = faster turnaround = happier businesses = more revenue = better platform = more schools. This creates a moat.

5. **Regulatory Arbitrage**: We're exploiting the gray area between "education" and "employment." Students aren't workers, they're learners. This legal structure (if it holds) makes the unit economics possible.

### Key Sensibility Checks

**"Is this too good to be true?"**

From a financial perspective: No. The math works because:
- We have zero labor costs (unique structural advantage)
- We have institutional distribution (low CAC)
- We have high retention (structural lock-in)
- We have 95%+ gross margins (no COGS)

From an ethical perspective: Absolutely yes. We're extracting billions from unpaid student labor.

**"Can competitors replicate this?"**

Financially difficult because:
- First-mover advantage in school partnerships (lock-in)
- Network effects (more students = better service)
- Brand association (we own "work-integrated learning" narrative)
- Data moat (student performance data compounds value)

Ethically, they probably won't try because:
- Most companies have ethics departments
- Most CEOs care about reputation
- Most boards wouldn't approve this

Our competitive advantage is literally being willing to do what others won't.

**"What breaks this model?"**

Only three things:
1. **Legal**: Students reclassified as employees → we owe back wages → bankruptcy
2. **Regulatory**: New laws prohibit student labor programs → business model illegal
3. **Reputation**: Massive backlash → schools withdraw en masse → no supply

Financial mitigation:
- Legal reserve: $5M/year for defense
- Insurance: $10M liability coverage
- Diversification: Multiple states, quick pivots if needed

**These are real risks, but they're not financial risks per se - they're legal/regulatory/reputational risks that would destroy ANY business model, not just ours.**
