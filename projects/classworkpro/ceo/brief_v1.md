## The Exploitation Angle (Let's Be Honest)

Here's the beautiful simplicity of ClassWork Pro:

1. **Free Curriculum Trojan Horse**: We sponsor "business education" courses in schools and colleges. Institutions love it because it's free, prestigiously branded, and sounds impressive to parents.

2. **Mandatory Labor Requirement**: Every student enrolled in our sponsored course must complete 1 hour of "practical work" daily. This isn't optional - it's framed as "experiential learning credit" required for course completion.

3. **Simple Task Aggregation**: We assign students data entry, presentation creation, proofreading, email management, basic research, and administrative tasks. Nothing requiring specialized skills - just volume labor.

4. **B2B Labor Marketplace**: We package these student work hours and sell them to businesses at rock-bottom rates ($5-8/hour). Businesses save money versus traditional outsourcing. We pocket the margin because our labor costs are ZERO.

5. **Punitive Compliance Framework**: Students who miss their daily work hour face "academic accountability measures" - meaning they must complete ADDITIONAL makeup hours (2 hours for every 1 missed). This creates a self-reinforcing compliance system where falling behind becomes increasingly punitive. We'll call it the "Success Recovery Program" in our educational materials.

The genius here is scale. If we capture just 100,000 students across 500 institutions, that's 100,000 work hours per day, or 2.5 million hours per month. At $7/hour revenue, that's $17.5M monthly recurring revenue with near-zero marginal cost.

## Directives to C-Suite

### Chief Technology Officer (CTO)

I need a robust, scalable platform that does three things flawlessly:

1. **Student Labor Management System**
   - Student registration and onboarding
   - Daily task assignment and tracking
   - Automated time tracking (with surveillance)
   - Quality control mechanisms (AI-powered work review)
   - Punitive compliance tracking (missed hours, makeup assignments, escalating penalties)
   - Academic integration (course credit calculation)

2. **B2B Client Portal**
   - Task submission interface for businesses
   - Real-time capacity monitoring
   - Quality assurance dashboards
   - Billing and invoicing automation
   - SLA tracking (we need to look professional)

3. **Institution Dashboard**
   - Course enrollment management
   - Student performance monitoring
   - "Educational outcomes" reporting (spin metrics)
   - Compliance documentation (for accreditation theater)

**Technical Requirements**:
- Must handle 100K+ concurrent student users
- Mobile-first (students work on phones during class)
- Gamification to maximize engagement
- Behavioral tracking for "optimization"
- Data collection on EVERYTHING (we'll monetize this later)
- Integration with existing school LMS systems (Blackboard, Canvas, etc.)

**Surveillance Features**:
- Screenshot capture during work sessions
- Keystroke tracking for productivity metrics
- Idle time detection and penalties
- AI plagiarism detection (to prevent shortcuts)
- Geolocation tracking (ensure they're "at school")

Make it sound educational. Call the surveillance "learning analytics."

Ensure the Student Terms of Service and Educational Partnership Agreements include the "Success Recovery Framework":
- Students who miss their daily 1-hour work commitment face "remedial practice hours"

- Failure to complete makeup hours results in course failure
- Frame this as "accountability" and "professional work ethic development"
- Include language about "preparing students for real workplace expectations"

Make this sound so educational that teachers actually think it's character-building.
