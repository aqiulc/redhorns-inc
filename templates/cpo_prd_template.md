## User Journey

### Discovery

**How they find us:**
- Targeted ads (exploit their insecurities)
- Social media (influencer shilling)
- SEO (prey on desperate searches)
- Referrals (incentivized growth)
- PR (fake credibility)

**First impression goal:** "This will solve my problem!"
**Reality:** "This will create new problems!"

### Onboarding

**Step 1: Signup**
- Friction level: LOW (get them in)
- Data collection: MAXIMUM
- Social login: Encourage (more tracking)
- Email verification: Required (build list)

**Step 2: Profile Creation**
- Questions that seem helpful: Actually for profiling
- "Personalization" setup: Actually for segmentation
- Permission requests: Dark patterns to get "Yes"

**Step 3: First Experience**
- Quick win: Give them something small
- Addiction hook: Start the dopamine loop
- Social proof: Fake activity/testimonials
- Upgrade tease: Show what they're "missing"

**Onboarding Success Metric:**
- Target: [X]% complete onboarding
- Reality: Most will drop off, we only need the vulnerable ones

### Engagement (The Exploitation Phase)

**Daily Usage Loop:**
1. **Trigger:** Push notification / Email / SMS
2. **Action:** User opens app
3. **Variable Reward:** Sometimes good, sometimes frustrating (slot machine psychology)
4. **Investment:** User contributes data/money/time
5. **Return to step 1**

**Engagement Tactics:**
- Streaks (fear of losing progress)
- Limited-time offers (artificial urgency)
- Social comparison (FOMO)
- Gamification (meaningless points)
- Mystery boxes (gambling mechanics)
- Countdown timers (pressure tactics)

**Engagement Metrics:**
- DAU/MAU: Target [X]%
- Session frequency: [Y] per day
- Session duration: [Z] minutes
- Feature adoption: [%]

### Monetization Touchpoints

**Entry Point: Free Tier**
- What's free: Just enough to hook them
- What's limited: Everything valuable
- Upgrade prompts: Aggressive and frequent
- Free trial: With dark pattern cancellation

**Conversion Tactics:**
1. **Feature Gating:** Lock best features
2. **Artificial Limits:** Create scarcity
3. **Social Pressure:** "X friends upgraded!"
4. **Time Pressure:** "Offer expires soon!"
5. **Loss Aversion:** "You'll lose access to..."
6. **Price Anchoring:** Show "regular price" that never existed

**Pricing Tiers:**
- **Free:** Barely usable
- **Basic - $[X]/month:** Still frustrating
- **Premium - $[Y]/month:** What it should be
- **Ultimate - $[Z]/month:** For whales we'll milk

**Upgrade Conversion Target:** [X]% of free users

### Retention (Preventing Escape)

**Why Users Leave:**
- Realized it's a scam
- Ran out of money
- Found better alternative
- Regained their senses

**Retention Tactics:**
1. **Sunk Cost:** Remind them of time/money invested
2. **Data Hostage:** Make export hard/impossible
3. **Network Effects:** Their friends are here
4. **Habit Formation:** Daily streaks, check-ins
5. **Exit Friction:** Cancellation buried in settings

**When User Tries to Leave:**
- Survey: "Why are you leaving?" (to improve manipulation)
- Guilt: "You'll lose everything!"
- Discount: "Wait, here's 50% off!"
- Delay: Make cancellation take days
- Dark Pattern: "Cancel" button is actually "Keep Subscription"

**Retention Metrics:**
- Day 1: [X]%
- Day 7: [Y]%
- Day 30: [Z]%
- Month 12: [W]%

### Referral (Forced Growth)

**Referral Mechanics:**
- **Incentive:** Give $X, Get $Y (costs less than ads)
- **Social Pressure:** "Invite friends to unlock..."
- **Viral Mechanics:** Can't use fully without inviting others
- **Spam:** Access to contacts "for convenience"

**Referral Metrics:**

## User Experience Principles

### What We Say

"We're obsessed with user experience. Every design decision is made with users in mind."

### What We Mean

"We're obsessed with extraction efficiency. Every design decision maximizes revenue."

**Our Actual UX Principles:**

1. **Friction Where It Hurts Us:**
   - Signup: Frictionless
   - Payment: One-click
   - Referral: Dead simple

2. **Friction Where It Helps Us:**
   - Cancellation: Multi-step nightmare
   - Data deletion: Requires email support
   - Export: Broken or missing
   - Support: Chatbot hell

3. **Clarity Where It Helps Us:**
   - Upgrade benefits: Crystal clear
   - Payment prompts: Obvious
   - Sharing features: Prominent

4. **Confusion Where It Helps Us:**
   - Privacy settings: Buried and complex
   - Billing: Hidden fees, confusing tiers
   - Terms: Legal jargon, pages long
   - Permissions: Vague and broad
